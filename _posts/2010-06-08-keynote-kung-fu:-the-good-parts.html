During the presentation, I summarized the 12 easy lessons that will have you on your way to becoming a Keynote Kung-Fu Master. Here they are in case you missed it.

Tell as Story

Not just any story. Tell your audience the story of your interface. Walk them through the experience from a user's perspective. Divide your wireframes up by use case or functional area and dive into each one.

Keynote is so perfect for this because in additional to a powerful wireframe tool, it's presentation capabilities can add a dimension of time. You are no longer limited to a static description of your interface. The story of your interface will be so much more powerful than boring documentation.

Visually narrate the interface details

Imagine that your wireframes are a flip-book. Walk your audience through each interaction frame-by-frame.

I call out each interaction using annotations like click, pinch, swipe, tap, etc. Your audience will clearly see the interaction design details of your wireframe, even if you aren't presenting it first-hand.

Use master slides for page layouts/types

This lesson is a game changer, or at least, it was for me. Avoid endless cut-and-paste by putting static elements, like navigation, backgrounds, and site “chrome” in master slides.

Reference the common elements in your slides simply by changing the master slide.

Create a library of components

Copy/Paste style properties to save time

Group items whenever possible

Learn the keyboard shortcuts

Tables are for more than just data

Add text to shape layer whenever possible

Keep your theme lean

Swap between Photoshop/Illustrator with ease
